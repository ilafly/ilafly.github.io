### About me

Currently, I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the University of Exeter (UK). I am working on the EPSRC project: Hopf-Galois Theory and Skew Braces (PI: <a href="http://emps.exeter.ac.uk/mathematics/staff/NPByott" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Prof. N. Byott</a>).

Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Vrije Universiteit Brussel (VUB) in the group <a href="https://we.vub.ac.be/nl/algb-algebra-incidence-geometry" target="_blank" rel="noopener">ALGB: Algebra, Incidence Geometry</a> (Group and Semigroup Theory) headed by <a href="http://we.vub.ac.be/en/eric-jespers" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Prof. E. Jespers</a>.

I completed my PhD in 2017 at the <a href="https://www.unisalento.it/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">University of Salento</a> under the supervision of <a href="https://www.unisalento.it/scheda-utente/-/people/francesco.catino/biografia" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Prof. F. Catino</a>. During my PhD, I was a visiting PhD student at the University of Warsaw in the group of <a href="https://www.mimuw.edu.pl/en/struktura/10010300" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Algebra and Number Theory</a> headed by <a href="https://www.mimuw.edu.pl/osoba/416" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Prof. J. Okniński</a>.

My PhD thesis focuses on studying a novel algebraic structure, namely semi-brace, and its connection with set-theoretical solutions of the Yang-Baxter equation.

My research interest focuses on studying algebraic structures associated with discrete versions of some equations in mathematical physics, such as the Yang-Baxter equation and the Pentagon equation. I am mainly interested in algebraic structures such as skew braces, trusses and generalisations that organise, classify and help to find solutions of the Yang-Baxter equation and the Pentagon equation with given properties.
